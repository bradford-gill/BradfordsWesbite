# The 2026 Red Sox: Gotta Enjoy Every Season

The much-anticipated Red Sox season is not going the way many Red Sox fans and baseball analysts thought it would be. Many players are significantly underperforming projections, and our two most prominent young stars, Garrett Crochet and Roman Anthony, are on the IL. Despite all this, for better or worse, I still find myself tuning in. This season is a great reminder that it is almost always better to be optimistic and wrong than pessimistic and correct. I have been trying to focus on the good rather than the team OPS, wOBA, or record. A couple things that have been bringin me joy are the tarps off crowd energy, Payton Tolle, a blue-chip prospect on the way, and Fenway on a sunny day.

## Tarps Off

If you follow MLB any amount it would be hard not to hear about tarps off. On the off chance you have missed the news, tarps off is when a section of fans, usually in the bleachers and so far just men, take their shirts off and cheer loudly. In fact they are so loud they can frequently be heard on the broadcasts. This is by no means exclusive to the Red Sox, it is taking MLB by storm but it is fun none-the-less.

## Payton Tolle

In terms of on-field quality there have been a few things that have been fun to watch. The young Connelly Early is developing into a valuable rotation piece. Off-season additions Sonny Gray, Ranger Suarez, and Wilson Contreras have been a blast to watch. The young Caleb Durbin had a very rough start offensively but seems to be working hard and improving his bat, and according to Baseball Savant he has been an above-average defensive third baseman. The bullpen has also been among the best in the league, not that it matters much when the offense is among the worst.

None of these players hold a candle to the joy the goofy 6'7" mustached Payton Tolle on the mound brings. The 23-year-old is in his second year of pro ball, in 2025 he rocketed from Single-A to the majors. His underlying numbers are promising, with an a[above-average fastball and good breaking stuff](https://baseballsavant.mlb.com/savant-player/payton-tolle-801139?stats=statcast-r-pitching-mlb). If he develops his off-speed stuff he has the potential to be a true ace. His FIP, which indicates future ERA better than a pitcher's ERA itself, is just 3.3, and his actual ERA is just 3.08.

He is certainly young and unpolished. He has made some silly plays, most noticeably a [ridiculous balk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3XGwxTnJwH0&t=1s). He played it off with grace and took responsibility, hopefully he got that style of balk out of his system.

Unfortunately he has gotten some of the worst run support of any pitcher, and his starts haven't resulted in as many wins as someone with his skills deserves.

This guy has potential, no doubt. I am looking forward to what he does over the next couple of years and hopefully decades.

## Franklin Arias — Something to Look Forward To

Franklin Arias is the brightest young prospect in the Red Sox system, maybe even the minor league system as a whole. Born in Venezuela, the 20-year-old is currently ripping up Double-A competition, batting .315 with an OPS of .974. Additionally, his [scouting report](https://www.mlb.com/milb/prospects/2024/redsox/franklin-arias-808265#:~:text=With%20quick%20feet%2C%20dependable%20hands,an%20easy%20right-handed%20swing.) says he has a great arm and good fielding. Hopefully he fills out over the next few years and can hit for power. His ETA is 2027, by which time he will be just 21, giving the Red Sox a potential franchise shortstop for years to come.

## Fenway Park When the Sun Is Out

Even in a losing season Fenway Park has a great atmosphere and a lot of extra charm. Nothing is better than watching the game with an overpriced beer and hot dog, and when the visiting team inevitably takes the lead do not hesitate to spend another $20 on another beer, just enjoy the sunset and maybe join the tarps off section.
